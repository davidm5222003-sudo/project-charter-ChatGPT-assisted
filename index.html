1. Project Title & Description

Project Title: Preparing for an Entry-Level IT Job

Project Goal: To become fully prepared for an entry-level IT position by developing relevant technical skills, professional competencies, and industry knowledge. This will be achieved through applied projects, certifications, and professional portfolio materials that demonstrate job readiness and adaptability across multiple IT roles.

Project Description: This project focuses on building both technical and professional capabilities needed to secure an entry-level position in the IT field. It includes researching industry expectations, developing essential IT and problem-solving skills through hands-on projects, and creating a professional portfolio with an updated resume and LinkedIn profile. By the end of the semester, I will be equipped to pursue and succeed in an entry-level IT role with confidence and competence.

Key Deliverables: Conduct interviews with professionals in relevant IT careers, complete research documentation, and produce updated resume, LinkedIn profile, and a professional portfolio website. These materials will demonstrate my understanding of industry expectations and readiness for entry-level IT positions.

2. Vision, Mission & Strategy Alignment

Personal Mission Connection:

This project supports my personal mission to have a work and impact that I am committed to, that I find interesting, and that provides value to help people. The values this project reflects are to help others and to provide meaningful value.

Completing this project moves me towards my long-term vision for my career as it enhances my skills and ability to provide value and help others. This project is related to a career that I find interesting and aligns with my long-term vision of working towards a career that I find interesting.

8. Project Methodology

I believe that after looking at all the methodologies, the one that best fits my project is the Agile methodology. My main reason is that the project mirrors the factors of an agile methodology with a high level of uncertainty and volatility that is likely to change throughout the project‚Äôs progression. There are requirements that I planned and would like to meet, but those requirements have been flexible even in past planning. I was heavily considering hybrid-incremental or hybrid-iterative, but the issue is that I realized that I needed not only to refine and improve my deliverables, but also to add new goals within those deliverables as the project progresses, which would best fit an Agile method. My WBS also heavily reflects the rapid iterations and continuous adaptation of my project.

9. Project Tool Selection

I tried Microsoft Project, Jira, and Trello. I ultimately chose Jira. This is because it was one of the tools that fit my methodology and fit my needs the most. The board was the feature I liked a lot, so that was one of the main parts I liked about not only Jira but Trello as well. Trello, unfortunately, while very clean, is too simple without paying for more features. Being able to add apps to increase features like Microsoft 365 or a calendar feature was nice. There was also a timeline showing when tasks needed to be done. There is a page section that could also be useful to have information. Trello and Jira were both free, and Microsoft Project, I have access to as a student, so I wouldn‚Äôt have to pay for any of them, but Jira and Trello both have features locked behind paywalls, with Trello having more locked than Jira, as someone working on their own. This helped me pick Jira.
